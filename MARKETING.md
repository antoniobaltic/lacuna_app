# Lacuna — Brand & Marketing Strategy

## The Name

**Lacuna** — Latin for "a gap, a void, an empty space." A lacuna is something missing that should be there. The time between sealing and opening is a lacuna in your life — a gap filled with anticipation.

- Short, two syllables, easy to say globally
- Latin root understood across European languages
- Rare in the App Store — distinctive
- `l a c u n a` with our tracking is visually striking
- Intellectual without being pretentious
- "Someone sent me a lacuna" is mysterious and inviting

## The Vibe

Lacuna isn't a utility — it's a philosophy app disguised as a time capsule.

The onboarding tells the story: "you exist only now" → a moon rotates → a clock ticks → we ask for permission → we offer more → "capture it now." Each screen is a moment. The geometric shapes drift like constellations. Comets streak through. Everything breathes.

**The emotional core**: you exist only now. The version of you that seals this capsule will be different from the version that opens it. That's not sad — that's what makes it meaningful.

The creme-and-black palette, the celestial imagery, the whisper texts that say things like "it will be fine" and "you got time, still" — this is an app that speaks to the user personally, quietly, like a friend who understands time.

## Target Customers

**Primary**: 20-35 year olds into self-improvement, journaling, mindfulness. Uses Notion. Reads philosophy. Has a "digital detox" routine. Doesn't want another dopamine app.

**Secondary**: Couples and close friends who want something more intimate than a text. "I sealed something for you. Open it on your birthday."

**Tertiary**: Creatives — writers, photographers — sealing work behind time. "I wrote this at 25. Open at 30."

## Marketing Angle

### "the app that makes you wait."

Counterintuitive hook. In a world of instant everything, Lacuna deliberately makes something inaccessible. That's interesting enough to write about, tweet about, film TikToks about.

### Taglines
- **"no peeking."** — core constraint as a tagline
- **"you exist only now."** — philosophical, from the onboarding
- **"the gap between now and then."** — the literal meaning of lacuna
- **"seal it. forget it. feel it later."** — emotional, descriptive

### Visual Strategy

- **Creme and black product shots** — everything else in the App Store is colorful. We stand out by being almost monochrome.
- **Minimalist App Store listing** — clean, spare, typographic
- **Show the onboarding** — the "you exist only now" → "do I?" moment is viral on its own
- **Film the reveal** — the moment content dissolves into view after weeks/months of waiting

### The Social/Viral Loop

1. User A creates a capsule for User B
2. Shares `.capsule` file via iMessage/AirDrop/WhatsApp
3. User B installs Lacuna to open it
4. User B is now a user, sends their own capsules
5. Repeat

"Someone sealed a secret for me and I have to wait 6 months" — TikTok gold. Works because the app looks serious, not playful. The tension is real.

## Positioning

**Premium niche play.** Fewer downloads than a cutesy app, but higher conversion because the people who find us are our exact audience. The aesthetic signals quality. They're more likely to pay.

The creme minimal aesthetic IS our moat. Nobody else has built a time capsule app that feels like this.

## Content Ideas

- **TikTok/Reels**: Film sealing a message, show countdown, film opening. The onboarding itself is content.
- **Twitter/X**: "There's an app that locks messages behind time and you literally cannot peek. The wait is unbearable and I love it."
- **Reddit**: r/apps, r/iphone, r/selfimprovement
- **Product Hunt**: Monochrome screenshots dominate. Focus on "no peeking" and the philosophical angle.
- **Press**: "In a world of instant gratification, this app bets on anticipation and existential reflection."
